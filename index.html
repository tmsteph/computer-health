Computers Are Making Us Sick, Tired & Depressed

Here is the science — and what humanity can do about it

1 — The Biological Mismatch

Human biology evolved for movement, sunlight, and outdoor horizons — not screens. Modern computing reverses this, and the nervous system treats it as stress.

  • Blue light suppresses melatonin and disrupts sleep: National Institutes of Health (NIH)
  • Prolonged screen use causes eye strain (“Computer Vision Syndrome”): American Academy of Ophthalmology
  • Sitting more than 6 hours a day increases risk of heart disease and metabolic illness: CDC — Sedentary Behavior

2 — The Psychological Overload

Computers fight for mental bandwidth. Notifications, ads, and infinite feeds overload the brain’s dopamine system.

  • Social media triggers dopamine reward pathways like addictive drugs: NIH
  • Multitasking reduces IQ and productivity: American Psychological Association
  • Digital overload is linked to anxiety, depression, and burnout: World Health Organization — Mental Health

3 — The Loneliness Problem

Digital connection has replaced physical connection — but the nervous system needs real human presence.

  • Loneliness increases mortality as much as smoking: American Psychological Association
  • Online interaction does not provide the same emotional reward as face-to-face contact: NIH
  • Excessive social media use correlates with depression in teens and adults: JAMA Pediatrics

4 — Designed for Addiction

Tech companies profit when users stay scrolling. Infinite feeds and notifications are engineered to manipulate dopamine and behavior.

  • Infinite scrolling was designed to create compulsive use: BBC Future
  • Apps exploit psychological vulnerabilities for profit: APA — Tech Addiction
  • Surveillance capitalism uses data to control behavior: Shoshana Zuboff — "The Age of Surveillance Capitalism"

5 — The Solution: Human-Centered Computing

Technology can be redesigned to work with the human body instead of against it:

  • E-ink & low-blue-light displays reduce eye strain — NIH Study
  • Movement-based, ergonomic workstations improve mood and focus — CDC Workplace Health
  • Nature exposure boosts mental health — Environmental Research
  • Decentralized, privacy-respecting tech protects human autonomy — Electronic Frontier Foundation

6 — A New Era of Natural Computing

A new generation of technology is emerging — focused on wellness, empowerment, and biological harmony.

  • Open hardware and RISC-V: riscv.org
  • Community-owned platforms: Matrix.org
  • Neuroscience of mindful tech: Center for Humane Technology

Technology should make humans healthier — not weaker.
